About

I am an electronic engineer interested in Technology, Space, Astronomy and Physics.

My work is with the Array Maintenance Group of the Atacama Millimeter/Submillimeter Array (ALMA) Observatory. My duty is to provide maintenance solutions to the Correlators and Backend subsystems of the array, ensuring high-level standards for each observation.

I hold a M.Sc degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Chile. My thesis was related to the design and development an Out-of-Equilibrium Experiment for the Cubesat platform. This type of experiment was the first of its kind and was carried by the Satellite of University of Chile for Aerospace Investigation (SUCHAI), which was also the first satellite of the University of Chile. See a video and read the full document here.

During my master and undergraduate studies I have worked as a Teacher Assistant in several courses of Electrical Engineering, Physics and Computer Sciences Departments. I also enjoy playing with a telescope and a DSLR camera for astrophotography and playing sports.
